About the 3D Telemedicine Initiative in Ghana

A groundbreaking collaboration between the Canniesburn Plastic Surgery Unit in Glasgow, UK, the Korle Bu Teaching Hospital in Accra, Ghana, and Microsoft Corporation, Redmond, USA, spearheaded the development of 3D telemedicine in Ghana. This innovative project aimed to bridge geographical barriers and enhance healthcare delivery in the field of plastic surgery.

Our Mission

The mission of this collaborative effort was to leverage cutting-edge 3D and communication technologies to provide expert consultation and support for patients in Ghana requiring specialized plastic surgery care. By enabling remote assessment and planning, the initiative sought to improve patient outcomes and reduce the need for extensive travel.

Developing 3D Telemedicine Capabilities

The development of 3D telemedicine in this context involved a synergistic approach from the participating institutions:

  1. Clinical Expertise (Canniesburn & Korle Bu): Surgeons from the Canniesburn Plastic Surgery Unit provided specialized knowledge and consultation, while clinicians at Korle Bu Teaching Hospital facilitated on-the-ground patient interaction and data collection.
  2. Technology Innovation (Microsoft): Microsoft contributed its expertise in 3D imaging, data processing, and secure communication platforms to enable the remote sharing and manipulation of patient-specific anatomical models.
  3. Remote Collaboration: The initiative focused on establishing robust and secure communication channels that allowed for real-time interaction between medical professionals in the UK and Ghana, facilitating joint case reviews and treatment planning.
  4. Capacity Building: A key aspect of the project was to build local capacity within Korle Bu Teaching Hospital to utilize these advanced telemedicine tools effectively and sustainably.

Key Innovations

Remote Consultation

Enabling specialist consultations across continents, breaking down geographical barriers to expert medical advice.

3D Imaging in Telemedicine

Pioneering the use of 3D models derived from patient data for enhanced remote diagnosis and surgical planning.
